"Monster's Den", a collection of poems about ugly emotions. Written by Brooklynne Toff

This work could have adult content or themes. If you continue reading, you have agreed that you are willing to see such content and themes.

When we test, we write one line

Then another, to be just fine

One more after, to make sure

Then a flourish, to be heard

Sadness

Clouds

The vast sky
Filled with
Tears that do not fall.

Insomnia

It says, "No."
Lids are heavy,
But they won't close.
Sleep has gone, Leaving blankets

Behind.


Outside, the wind sings.

Air
Passes through the glass screen,
As if it were not there.


Would it be strange to
Flip up the lock
And
Push?

Escape out into 2 A.M.
Onto cold pavement where
Only a lamppost will keep company?

Set foot on a
Never-ending street
With lights--not the color of the rainbow
Below which shadows flicker,
Dancing to songs that others
Cannot hear?


It says, "No."
Eyes wide open, staring blankly ahead.
Flower prints on the ceiling
Ears listening to the
Steady tune of
The clock, ticking on the shelf.

Stasis

Microscopic molecules
Shiver
Tremble
Merge
Then scatter at
A hiss of air.


Yellow beams glare
Angry
Stubborn
Forgotten
Caged by the gap
Between the exit
And the floor.


Translucent cirlces
Float
Dance
Suspended by its polycarbonate strings
Light
Lost
Dispersed.


Between these four walls
There is
Nothing.

Safe

Slam

Wake to see white

Why?

Why?
Clean to be dirty

Why?
Love, to hate more


But I liked it.
I know I did.
I remember.

Love

Tall and Long
Like a bean.

Chicken legs
Broad shoulders.

Short nails
Sparkling eyes.

I loved them all
For they were mine.

Thirst

You keep smiling
And I?
I--
I feel like
You have set me
On fire.

Confusion

Snow parts from the clouds,
Sparkling as they flutter in space,
Melting on your fingertips


Bare fingers in the snow
Should feel cold--your touch is warm?
How can that be? I don't know.


I part from your hold,
Stumbling as I drift into snow,
Sinking into the cold.

Long arms at my side,
Caging me in orchids and whispers,
Bringing me close.

Stubborn

Tired shakes
Fumbled buttons
Wet clothes
Soft lips
Bruising hips
Cracked skin


Rattling chest
I want to
I want you
Don't you believe me?

You

Small hands pat soaked hair
Clear eyes looking right through
A soul turned more than dark:
Transparent.

Silent

Panicked, not frantic
You diagnose.

Fear, not desire
You whisper.


Firm hands onShaking legs
Shh.

Still

But I do want you.
Some part of me does.
Here, and here.
I do.

You

"We can take it slow."

Me

Why are you so good to me?

Fatigue

A quiet harbor waits,
Battered by hail and storm.

A gentle place where
Harsh winds rest
And moons sleep.


After destruction rained and fell,
Tiles were flooded
And windows shattered,
You were still here.

All was well.

Anger

Today I heard some
Interesting news.

2% bad,
98% good,
100% not heard by you.


They came, in suits,
Bearing news.
Beards, neatly trimmed,
Eyes, finely sculpted,
More man than me for you.

They came, in dresses,
Smiling wide.
Nails, bejweled,
Lips, drawn pink,
More lady than you for me.


And as they reared their ugly heads,
You gave them your smile,
And I was angry.
And as they grinded with their soles,
You looked away,
And I was hurt.


Your small frame
Failed to shield
My large build.

Your paper angel
Was pierced through
By their gaze.

Did it hurt?


"You don't belong."
Wrong as it was,
You did not refute,
And I was angry.

I watched you smile
As they pronouced,
That I should leave you,
And I was hurt.


Why don't you speak?
Do you hear them
Ripping my heart,
Angel?

Do you see them
Twisting us apart,
Angel?

Frustration

Do you hurt
Like I do?

Do you shed tears

Like I do?

You are so perfect
How is this fair?
How can I protect you
If you never tear?


Perhaps your heart
Heals faster
So you have no fear.

Why do you feel no pain
When they hurt you
Why do you have no tears
When they hurt me?

How many times
Can you bear
Until you stop smiling?

Disgust

Broken
Left, laid down.

Cold.

Do not move.
Do not think.

If you disappeared,
Then so would they.


Then woods smelling like grass would re-appear,
Then the winds would run,
Then the seas would rock,
Then the skies would tremble,
Then the air would kiss,

Then I would breathe in chilled air.

Hate

I hate it
I hate the sunshine
The way it glares
I hate the halo
Around your hair
I hate balloons
The way they fly
They slip away
If they aren't tied.


I hate small hands
That hover by
I don't need you
When I cry


I hate the socks
I hate the tea
I hate the biscuits
I hate the sheets


I hate the quiet
I hate your voice
When it goes silent
I rejoice


I hate the warmth
I hate the care
When you let go
I only stare


I hate the front
That you put on
When I hurt you
You still go on


Why are you strong
When I am weak
Why can't I hurt you
When I speak?

Triumph

The ropes are untied
The curtain falls
Your act has ended.


(There's no applause.)

Bitter

Some things itch when
They are dry
That's why I cry

Choked

These tears are
Not for you
They are for these eyes
That have been open
Too wide

Yearning

Hot wine like your touch
Fleeting
In my throat

Warm air like your breath
Gentle
Exhale

The bottle tinkles on my ring
Like your laugh


I'm sorry
I didn't want you to leave
Where did you go?

Hot wine splashes
Like your tears
Burning my skin.

Fear

It wasn't me
Who hurt you
It was them
It wasn't me


Will you...come back?

Power

Angel, have you left me
Here in the snow
Covered in white light
Will you let me freeze?

Angel, do you really
Not want me
Anymore?


Don't worry...
Even if you are gone...
I won't leave you, I promise

Standing tall
2 feet apart
Not moving close.

Polite decline
To socialize
When they come near.

Step around

With ease.

What if...
Then if--

They think
They are smart
That they can
Bargain

And niceties,

Pure

Drown them in four walls
That have no corners.

Allow them to be grounded
Without a sky.

Erase the floor that bounds them
And their anchor.

Put away night, shade, cool air;
Bathe them in light.

Strip them of their memories;
Forget what is pain (and hope).


Then silently turn the lock
And leave them there in peace.

Alone.

Alive

Bend at my touch
And speak
Crackle and moan
Repeat

Drip, drop
Twist firmly like a clock
Squeeze

No sound, then
Spilling
Onto slim fingers.

Shadows blend,
Movements unnoticed
Dark

White lights flashing
Blinding closed lids

The world
Still spinning
Spinning
Roaring
Too fast
