My Personal Style: Visualizing Style

| August 01 2022 |

~ * Cute, Clean, Comfortable * ~

A look has three parts: the hair, the outfit, and the accessories. I want to be seen as neat, still youthful, and ready to get things done. I don't like giving a "lavish" or "trying-too-hard" impression. I also want to be comfortable; I'm usually always on the move. I want to feel like ME.

The Hair:

The default look

Thin bangs, curtain side bangs that end around the mouth, and long straight hair.

The easy look

A high/low ponytail, or a side ponlytail with a small braid.

The harder look that may require multiple pins and hair ties.

Two low buns, two side braides, two half side braid and half pig tails, or a long bubble braid.

The "Go To" Outfits

The casual look

A T-shirt or long sleeve, jeans, and runners.

The workout or lounge wear look

A T-shirt, pair of shorts, and slippers or runners.

The formal look

A blouse over a tank top or t-shirt. A sweater or blazer over that. A pair of slacks. Heeled boots, flats, or sneakers if dressing down.

The dressy look

Literally a dress, or a skirt with a blouse, and any kind of shoe to dress it up or down. Usually lighter colors for the day, darker colors for the night.

Finally, the accessories. There's a lot of freedom with accessories. Select ones that will give the extra touch to the outfit and complete it the way you want. Mix warm and cool tones, or stick to one for a more traditional look. Save glitzy pieces for the evening. Try a monochrome look, a "in-the-same-color-different-shade" look, or a contrasting look. Double check that the accessories are in good condition. Double check dress codes - both official and conventional ones.
