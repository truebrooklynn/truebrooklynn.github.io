My Personal Style: Capsule Wardrobe

| August 02 2022 |

A capsule wardrobe is small. Mine was not created out of desire. The main reason I have one is: not enough space. After moving into a studio apartment for rent taught me two things:

1. I don't have space for a large set of clothes

2. Having many clothes will make my next move even more of a headache.

Minimizing the amount of clothing I have while still always having something to wear for the right occasion is tough. There are multiple occasions I dress up for: casual for everyday errands (I am not doing groceries in a fancy shirt and shoes), chores, sleeping, working out, dressy for nights out and grabbing food with friends, and formal for the professional environment.

The other requirement is my wardrobe should work for fluctuating temperatures. There are 4 seasons where I live, with summers seeing up to 40 degrees celsius, and winters can be down to -30 degrees celsius. There's all kinds of precipitation: rain and snow.

Finally, being environmentally conscious to me includes reducing the use of electricity and water. This means I am not doing laundry every other day. There should be enough clothes to wear until my next laundry day.

My capsule wardrobe was not borne from an aspiration for the minimalistic lifestyle. It did not come from a wish to wear all neutral colors either. It came from the constraints of reality. It's a necessity. It's life.

Sustainability Tips

Treating your environment right = treating yourself right!

Love what you have! Fall in love with the things you know. Really get to know them: how to care for them, how they fit on you, what colors and shapes go well with them. Then use them. You bought them for a reason: they fit you, they are amazing, go and wear them. This is tricky in times of online shopping, however I have to say, the best is to try on before you buy and make sure it's comfortable and fits you right before you do the purchase. Finally, thrift - reuse before buying new. It's not embarrassing to thrift or wear second-hand, I promise!
