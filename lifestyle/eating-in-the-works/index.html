Eating in the Works

I am a firm believer of "you are what you eat". I also believe in "calories in, calories out" discipline as overconsumption not only adds uncomfortable bloating for me, but also adds unnecessary stress. Hence, I try to limit snacking and consuming food cooked outside (especially fried foods) as it is unsustainable for me. Although I do not think of myself as a good cook, I've lost count of the number of times I spent money on food outside, only to think of it as a waste later because I did not like the food and could have made it taste better for me, had I cooked it myself.

My top personal goals related to food are to limit my sugar and sodium intake and limit my fried food consumption with the hope to completely stop eating fried food on day. My key cooking appliances are: a rice cooker, a stovetop and oven, an air fryer, and a smoothie blender. My key food preparation tools are: a chopper and board, a grater, a garlic press, and a vegetable peeler.

I'm sharing my daily and weekly targets to stay on track for my goals. I would like to emphasize that these are my personal beliefs and these continue to change. I would recommend others to identify their own values and preferences and then sticking to it.

Daily

  • 6 - 8 cups of water
  • 1 - 2 cups of milk
  • 1 bone soup with leaf or root vegetables
  • 1 grain item (e.g., white rice, zucchini bread)
  • 1 serving of egg / tofu / poultry meat / red meat / seafood
  • Additional fruits and vegetables

Weekly

  • 1/2 cup of beans
  • 1 handful of nuts
  • 1 cup of vegetable or fruit juice
  • At least 4 meals with red meat in a week
  • Minimize consuming bread
  • Limit snacking, eating out / takeout, and processed foods
  • Limit added oil and salt
