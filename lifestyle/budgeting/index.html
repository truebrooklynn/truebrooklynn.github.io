Budgeting: do not break the bank for style.

Following a plan and a budget helps maximize long-term cohesiveness. It keeps my possessions a reusable and organized nature. They work as a team. This saves me from potentially wasting money, time, and effort on buying the same things more than once, or something that is completely incompatible with everything else I already own.

Wearing expensive clothes does not make you fashionable.

Each purchase and item I spend on are not intended to be a final outfit themselves, but rather the building blocks for later, and multiple, outfits. Minimizing owning unnecessary duplicates avoids wasting storage space, which is costly. Finally, there is no embarrassment in thrifting, nor in using or wearing non-brand named clothes.

My budgeting tools:

The Monthly Expense Tracker

A spreadsheet that displays expenses across categories to distinguish spending habits and identify targets of interest when creating a saving plan.

The Goal Setting Wish List

A wish list for mapping out things you want to provide help separating impulse purchases and what goals are worthy to pursue and the appropriate method to do so (thrift, DIY, buy).

The Wardrobe Box

An organizer for a holistic view on the things you own. Helps with building strategies to use and what needs to be replaced, as well as items that are not useful.
